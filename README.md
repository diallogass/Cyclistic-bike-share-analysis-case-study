## Objective
To understand how **annual members** and **casual riders** use Cyclistic bikes differently, and to use these insights to recommend marketing strategies that encourage membership growth.

## Dataset Used
- <a href="https://github.com/diallogass/Cyclistic-bike-share-analysis-case-study/blob/main/Cyclist%20Data%2C%20Marketing%20Analysis.xlsx">Project_Dataset<a/> 

## Data Cleaning and Preparation
To ensure the dataset was accurate and ready for analysis, the following cleaning and preparation steps were completed:

- Imported Cyclistic’s historical bike-share data into **Google Sheets**
- Separated **date** and **time** into individual columns
- Calculated **ride length** for each trip
- Created a **day-of-week** column to analyze weekday vs. weekend usage
- Removed **duplicate ride IDs** to maintain data integrity
- Organized the dataset for further descriptive analysis

## Analysis Process
After cleaning the data, descriptive analysis was performed using **formulas** and **pivot tables** in Google Sheets.

### Key analytical steps included:
- Calculating **average ride length**
- Counting the number of rides by rider type
- Identifying **trends by day of the week**
- Comparing usage behavior between:
  - **Annual members**
  - **Casual riders**
- Creating visualizations to support findings and reveal behavioral patterns

## Key Findings

The findings revealed clear behavioral differences between the two user groups.

- **Annual members** tend to take shorter but more frequent rides, primarily during weekdays, indicating that they use the service mainly for commuting purposes.
- **Casual riders** take longer rides and are more active on weekends, suggesting that they use the bikes for leisure and recreational activities.
- Although casual riders engage less frequently, their longer ride durations indicate strong interest and potential value.

## Recommended Marketing Strategies

Based on these insights, several marketing strategies are recommended:

- **Target casual riders on weekends:**  
  Cyclistic should target casual riders during weekends, when their activity is highest, by offering limited-time discounts or trial membership promotions.

- **Promote annual memberships for commuters:**  
  The company should position annual memberships as a cost-effective and convenient solution for daily commuting, highlighting savings and ease of use for regular riders.

- **Use behavior-based marketing:**  
  Cyclistic can implement behavior-based marketing by identifying frequent or high-duration casual riders and sending them personalized offers through email or in-app notifications.

- **Leverage digital media campaigns:**  
  Digital media campaigns should be leveraged to reinforce these messages, focusing on the benefits of unlimited access, flexibility, and long-term savings.

  
### Annual Members
- Take **shorter but more frequent rides**
- Are more active during **weekdays**
- Likely use the service primarily for **commuting**

### Casual Riders
- Take **longer rides**
- Are more active during **weekends**
- Likely use the service mainly for **leisure and recreational purposes**

**Note:** Although casual riders use the service less frequently, their **longer ride durations** suggest strong engagement and represent a valuable opportunity for conversion into annual members.

## Recommendations
Based on these insights, the following marketing strategies are recommended:

### 1. Target Casual Riders on Weekends
Casual riders are most active on weekends, making this the ideal time to promote conversion offers.

**Recommended actions:**
- Offer **limited-time weekend discounts**
- Introduce **trial membership promotions**
- Run **weekend-focused campaigns** at high-traffic locations

### 2. Position Annual Memberships for Commuters
Annual members show strong weekday commuting behavior, which suggests membership should be marketed as a practical daily solution.

**Recommended messaging:**
- Highlight **cost savings** over single rides
- Emphasize **convenience and flexibility**
- Promote membership as ideal for **daily commuting**

### 3. Use Behavior-Based Marketing
Cyclistic can identify casual riders who show strong usage patterns and target them with personalized conversion offers.

**Recommended actions:**
- Segment **frequent casual riders**
- Identify riders with **high average ride durations**
- Send **personalized emails** or **in-app notifications**
- Offer tailored promotions based on ride behavior

### 4. Leverage Digital Media Campaigns
Digital channels can reinforce membership benefits and support conversion efforts.

**Recommended campaign focus:**
- **Unlimited ride access**
- **Flexibility and convenience**
- **Long-term savings**
- Lifestyle messaging for both **commuters** and **weekend riders**

## Conclusion
This analysis demonstrates that **annual members** and **casual riders** use Cyclistic bikes in distinctly different ways. Members primarily use the service for weekday commuting, while casual riders are more engaged in longer, weekend leisure rides.
These insights provide a strong foundation for targeted marketing strategies aimed at converting casual riders into annual members. By focusing on timing, behavior, and personalized messaging, Cyclistic can improve membership growth and increase customer lifetime value.
